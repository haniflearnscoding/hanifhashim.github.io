Phasing Mario Block

3D animation exploring phasing, rhythm, and repetition

3D Animation Blender Experimental

About This Project

Phasing Question Blocks is a visual exploration of Steve Reich's pioneering phasing technique translated into 3D animation. Two identical Mario question blocks rotate at slightly different speeds, creating evolving visual patterns that mirror Reich's aural phasing compositions. The blocks gradually fall out of sync, creating subtle rhythmic variations before realigning at the loop's end—a visual ouroboros of endless transformation.

Concept & Inspiration

The inspiration came from composer Steve Reich's pioneering use of phasing in audio, where two identical loops gradually fall out of sync to create complex, shifting patterns. This piece translates that sonic technique into 3D graphics, exploring how the affordances of visual media—rotation, spatial relationships, and form—parallel Reich's exploration of pitch and rhythm in sound.

The work also engages with the broader context of phasing in video art, where the technique has been used for political commentary through uncanny patterns in speech and movement. Here, the familiar Mario question block becomes the subject, its endless rotation reflecting the ouroboros symbol—stability and change in perpetual cycle.

Visual Pattern & Structure

The two blocks rotate with different degrees of rotation over 1800 frames. A subtle visual pattern emerges as the blocks phase through different states of alignment, creating new composite shapes and forms. The pattern only fully realigns at the loop's end—the viewer identifies the halfway point by observing the regression in the pattern, revealing the loop's structure through the phasing relationship itself.

Technical Implementation

Created in Blender using manual keyframe animation. After exploring Python scripting and driver-based automation, the final workflow involved calculating rotation values in Excel across four columns (frame number, object A rotation, object B rotation, delta) and manually inserting 1800 frames of keyframe data. While labor-intensive, this approach provided precise control over the phasing relationship.

Technologies

  • Blender 3D (animation and rendering)
  • HDRI lighting for photorealistic illumination
  • Excel for rotation value calculation
  • Keyframe animation over 1800 frames

Context

Created for CART361 - Interactive Media II (Ouroboros assignment) at Concordia University, Fall 2024.
