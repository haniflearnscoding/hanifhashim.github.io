JMSB Graduation Booklet

Ceremony Program for Portfolio Management Graduates

Role Graphic Designer
Client Concordia University - John Molson School of Business
Year 2022

Project Overview

A graduation ceremony booklet designed for the Kenneth Woods Portfolio Management Program (KWPMP) Class of 2022, celebrating the achievements of specialized business students within Concordia's John Molson School of Business.

Challenge

Create an elegant, contemporary program booklet that reflects the prestige of the specialized portfolio management program while maintaining brand consistency with both the John Molson School of Business and Concordia University visual identities.

Solution

A sophisticated gradient-based design utilizing a cyan-to-purple color transition that creates a modern, financial-tech aesthetic. The cover features a pixelated gradient pattern reminiscent of data visualization and digital markets, establishing immediate visual interest. Clean typography and generous whitespace throughout the interior create a professional, easy-to-navigate program that honors both the formality of graduation and the innovation of portfolio management education.

Full booklet showing gradient cover design and interior program details

Design Approach

Gradient Aesthetic

A contemporary cyan-to-purple gradient creates visual sophistication while suggesting data flow and market dynamics

Pixelated Pattern

Geometric pixel grid overlay adds texture and references digital finance technologies central to modern portfolio management

Typography Hierarchy

Clear distinction between headings, subheads, and body text using color-coded typography for easy navigation during ceremony

Dual Branding

Seamless integration of both Concordia University and John Molson School of Business brand elements

Color & Visual Language

The gradient transitions from bright cyan through blues and purples to deep violet, creating a sense of progression—appropriate for a graduation celebrating students' journey from education to professional practice. The pixelated overlay pattern adds dimensionality without overwhelming the clean layout.

A vertical maroon accent bar on the right edge of the cover provides a subtle nod to Concordia's brand colors while maintaining the booklet's contemporary aesthetic. This accent repeats on the back cover, creating visual continuity across the piece.

Content Organization

The booklet systematically presents the ceremony's order of proceedings, beginning with welcoming remarks and progressing through remarks from university leadership (President Graham Carr, Dean Anne-Marie Croteau, program founder Kenneth Woods), a keynote address from Robert Sankey (CEO, Burgundy Asset Management), and culminating in the presentation of Class of 2022 graduates and scholarship awards.

Graduate names are presented with appropriate formality, and scholarship information includes both recipient names and award amounts, ensuring transparency and proper recognition. The interior uses a purple color scheme for headings that ties back to the gradient cover while maintaining excellent readability against white backgrounds.

Typography Strategy

The design employs a clean sans-serif system throughout. Section headings appear in a bold purple (matching the gradient theme), while body text uses neutral dark gray for optimal legibility. The cover typography is set in crisp white, creating strong contrast against the gradient background.

Special attention was paid to name presentation—all graduate and scholarship recipient names use consistent formatting to ensure equal prominence and respect for each individual's achievement.

Program Context

The Kenneth Woods Portfolio Management Program (KWPMP) is a specialized certificate program within the John Molson School of Business, focusing on advanced portfolio management techniques and professional development for finance careers. This graduation ceremony represents the culmination of intensive study in investment management, financial analysis, and portfolio strategy.

The design needed to honor this specialized focus while remaining accessible to the diverse audience attending the ceremony—including family members, university administrators, industry professionals, and program founders.

Scholarship Recognition

The booklet dedicates significant space to scholarship presentation, recognizing recipients of awards including the Bfinance Scholarship, David Abramson Scholarship, Donald Walcot Scholarship, Abraham Brodt Scholarship, and The Reena Atanasiadis Scholarship. Award values range from $2,000 to $3,500, representing substantial financial recognition of academic excellence.

This emphasis on scholarships highlights the program's commitment to supporting talented students and acknowledges the generosity of donors and industry partners who make these awards possible.

Print Execution

The booklet was designed as a multi-page saddle-stitched document with careful attention to gradient reproduction in print. Ensuring smooth gradient transitions without banding required precise color management and high-quality printing specifications. The pixel pattern was optimized to maintain clarity at print resolution while avoiding moiré effects.

Impact & Purpose

This booklet serves as both functional ceremony guide and commemorative keepsake. For graduates, it's a permanent record of their achievement and recognition within an elite finance program. For the university, it demonstrates the caliber of specialized programs offered through the John Molson School of Business.

The contemporary design aesthetic signals that KWPMP graduates are prepared for modern financial markets—comfortable with technology, data-driven decision-making, and innovative approaches to portfolio management.
